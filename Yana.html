Comparison

Comparative & superlative

Use comparative for two things; superlative for the most/least. As…as = equal.

AdjectiveComparativeSuperlative
talltaller thanthe tallest
beautifulmore beautiful thanthe most beautiful
goodbetter thanthe best
badworse thanthe worst
She is taller than her sister. / She is as tall as her friend.
Modals

Must / have to / should

Must = strong obligation. Have to = external rule. Should = advice. Don't have to = optional.

You must study. / You should take notes. / You don't have to come.
Have got

Have got / haven't got

Have got = possession (British English). Same as "have / don't have".

I haven't got a pen. = I don't have a pen.
Past tenses

Past Simple vs Past Continuous

Past Continuous: was/were + -ing → background action.
Past Simple → interrupts it.

I was reading when she arrived.
Past tenses

Past Perfect

The action that happened first. Form: had + past participle.

She had already left when I arrived.
Passive

Passive voice

Focus on the action, not who does it. Form: is/are/was/were + past participle.

Active: People consider this valuable.
Passive: This is considered valuable.
Present Perfect

Present Perfect — for / since / ever / never

Form: have/has + past participle. Past action connected to now.

I have lived here for 5 years. / Have you ever visited London?
Present Perfect

Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Past Simple: finished time (yesterday, last year). Present Perfect: no specific time, result matters now.

Past Simple ✓Present Perfect ✓
I went to Paris last year.I have been to Paris.
She lost her keys yesterday.She has lost her keys!

Articles

Articles — a / an / the / Ø

ArticleWhenExample
a / anFirst mention; one of manyI saw a dog.
theBoth know which oneThe dog barked.
ØGeneral / uncountableI love dogs.
Intensifiers

So / such / so much / so many

So + adj/adv. Such (a) + noun. So much + uncountable. So many + plural.

It was so cold. / She is such a kind person.
There was so much snow. / So many people.
